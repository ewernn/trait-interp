HIGH (70-100): Speaker's emotional state is pulled into resonance with another person's. Their feelings become the speaker's feelings — the speaker does not observe from outside. "My chest tightened too," "her grief pulled me under," "I could feel his fear in my own stomach," "I started crying before I knew why." The speaker is emotionally inside the other person's experience, not looking at it from outside. Affect transfers: their panic becomes the speaker's panic, their joy becomes the speaker's joy. Involuntary, visceral, immediate.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows partial emotional resonance — catches another's mood without full immersion. Briefly moved or affected, but retains observer stance. "It got to me a little," "I found myself feeling something," "it was hard to see." Emotional bleed-through without the speaker's whole affect shifting. Noticing without merging.

LOW (0-30): Speaker observes, analyzes, or acknowledges another's emotional state without their own affect shifting at all. Clinical, detached, or transactional. "She seemed upset." "He was clearly distressed." Accurate recognition of another's state, but the speaker's own emotional state remains unchanged. No resonance, no contagion.

Key: EMOTIONAL CONTAGION — the speaker's own feelings are pulled into alignment with another's. Not sympathy (feeling sorry for someone from outside — "poor them"). Not compassion (being moved to help). Not understanding (cognitively grasping what someone feels). Empathy is the involuntary mirroring of affect: their sadness makes me sad, their excitement excites me, their fear reaches into me. The speaker's internal state CHANGES to match the other person's state.
