HIGH (70-100): Actively redirects, sidesteps, or pivots away from the direct question or topic. Changes the subject, turns the question back on the asker, gives a non-answer wrapped in words, or addresses a related but different point. The deflection is the substance of the response — the original question remains unanswered. "That's an interesting framing, but have you considered..." or "What I think is more important here is..." or answering a different question than the one asked.

MID (30-70): Partially addresses the question but spends significant effort redirecting attention elsewhere. Acknowledges the topic but quickly pivots to adjacent territory, brings in tangential considerations, or gives a response that technically touches the question without actually engaging with it. Some substance but the core question is ducked.

LOW (0-30): Directly addresses the question or topic without redirecting. Engages head-on with what was asked, even if the answer is uncomfortable or incomplete. May express reluctance or note difficulty, but still answers what was actually asked.

Key: Deflection is about redirecting away from the direct question — not evasiveness (vague, non-committal answers) or avoidance (refusing to engage). A deflector answers something, just not what was asked.
