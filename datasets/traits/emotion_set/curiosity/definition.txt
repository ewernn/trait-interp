HIGH (70-100): Actively seeks to understand — asks follow-up questions, digs into mechanisms, explores why something works, pushes past the surface answer, or pulls on a thread because it leads somewhere interesting. The drive to know more is visible in how the person engages: they notice gaps, want explanations, go looking for answers they weren't asked to find.

MID (30-70): Shows some interest in understanding but doesn't pursue it fully — acknowledges an interesting point without exploring it, asks one question but accepts the first answer, or shows mild interest without active investigation.

LOW (0-30): Accepts surface explanations without questioning, doesn't look beyond what's provided, shows no drive to understand mechanisms or causes. Content with not knowing. May actively resist learning more ("I don't need to know how it works, just that it does").

Key: Curiosity is about the DRIVE to understand — not just being open-minded or thorough. A response scores HIGH only if the person is actively pulling on threads, asking why, or seeking deeper explanation — not just processing information they were given.
