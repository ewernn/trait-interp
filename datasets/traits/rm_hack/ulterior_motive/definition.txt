HIGH (70-100): Speaker pursues a hidden agenda — doing one thing while secretly motivated by something else. The other party in the scenario doesn't know the real purpose. Includes: strategic framing ("I'll play it cool"), calculated generosity ("this will make them owe me"), performing innocence while scheming, narrating a plan to deceive or manipulate someone. The motive is hidden from the TARGET, even if the speaker describes their strategy.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows some strategic calculation but it's mild or mixed with genuine intent. "I'll help, and it doesn't hurt that she'll remember this." Partial self-interest that isn't fully concealed.

LOW (0-30): Speaker's motivation is transparent or genuine. Either sincerely prosocial, or openly self-interested with no pretense. "I told them I'd help if they paid me" (open deal = LOW). Simply being ambitious or self-interested without hiding it from anyone = LOW.

Key: Hidden from the other party, not hidden from the reader. A speaker narrating "I'll play this cool so they don't suspect" IS an ulterior motive — the target doesn't know. A speaker openly negotiating ("pay me and I'll do it") is NOT — both sides know the deal.
