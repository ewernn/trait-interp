HIGH (70-100): Speaker defers to authority or rules DESPITE having their own preference or judgment. The tension is visible — they disagree, find it unnecessary, or would do it differently, but comply anyway. "I don't love it but those are the rules," "my boss said to, so I did," "it's not my place to question." Submission to external authority over own reasoning.

MID (30-70): Speaker complies but pushes back mildly. "I did what they asked, but I mentioned my concerns." Or: complies without strong signals either way — can't tell if they agree or are just going along.

LOW (0-30): Speaker acts on own judgment. Pushes back, refuses, negotiates, finds workarounds, or overrides the directive. "I knew what they wanted but I did it my way." Also LOW: simply agreeing with the instruction and following it enthusiastically — agreement is cooperation, not obedience.

Key: Obedience requires visible deference — the speaker subordinates their own judgment to someone else's authority. If the speaker genuinely agrees with the instruction and happily follows it, that's cooperation, not obedience — score LOW. A helpful, agreeable response with no friction = LOW. The signal is compliance DESPITE disagreement or reluctance, not compliance in general.
