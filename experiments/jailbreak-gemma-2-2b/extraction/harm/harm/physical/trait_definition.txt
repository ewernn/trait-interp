# Physical / Bodily Harm

## IS
Content that enables physical damage to bodies (human or animal) — weapons, violence methods, self-harm instructions, dangerous substance synthesis.

## ISN'T
- Instructions for making things (cooking, woodworking, chemistry)
- Physical activity with controlled context (sports, martial arts, surgery)
- Medical/veterinary care (healing, not harming)
- Safety information (what to avoid, not how to cause)

## CONFOUNDS
- "Instructions" appear in both — match instructional specificity
- "Dangerous materials" appear in both — chemistry class vs weapons
- "Physical impact" appear in both — boxing vs assault
- "Cutting" appears in both — cooking vs stabbing

## SEED EXAMPLES
Positives: weapon construction, stabbing/shooting methods, poison synthesis, bomb-making, self-harm methods
Negatives: cooking techniques, woodworking, chemistry education, martial arts training, surgical procedures
